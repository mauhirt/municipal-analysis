# Table 0 — Cox Survival Analysis (Widmann 2026 complement)

Generated by `pipeline/analysis_table0_survival.py`. Two time-to-first-event
Cox proportional hazards models, complementing the Table 1 v2 panel LPM
results. Methodologically motivated by Widmann (2026).

## Setup

- **Event A:** first `Green_Bond_Issued == 1` per city. 72 events, 562 cities.
- **Event B:** first `Y_self_green == 1` per city. 57 events, 567 cities.
- **Framework:** `lifelines.CoxTimeVaryingFitter` with start-stop format;
  cities drop out of risk set after first event; never-issuers censored 2025.
- **Strata:** `state_id` (absorbs state-level baseline hazard — Widmann
  equivalent to OECD stratification).
- **Covariates:** full primary RHS from Table 1 v2 (Dem_Mayor + constituency
  + fiscal + compulsion + state environment + Widmann infrastructure
  accumulation + controls), all time-varying where meaningful.

**Note on scale:** `pres_dem_two_party_share_lag2` is measured on the 0-1
share scale, so an HR of ≈ 15 reflects the hazard ratio for moving from
0% Dem to 100% Dem. Converted to a 10-percentage-point swing,
exp(log(15.5) × 0.10) ≈ **1.31** for Cox A and ≈ **1.47** for Cox B — a
10-pp shift in Dem constituency raises the hazard of first municipal
green-bond entry by 31% (GBI) or 47% (self-green). Substantively large.

## Three key findings

### 1. Dem_Mayor is null in Cox too — consistent with the LPM result
`Dem_Mayor` has HR ≈ 1.4 (direction-positive, Dems faster to enter) but
does not clear p < 0.10 in either event-type. This matches Table 1 v2's
null extensive-margin LPM finding. The partisan gap at first-entry is
not detectable in a Cox framework either.

### 2. Constituency (pres_dem_vote_share) is the dominant partisan driver
The raw Dem two-party share coefficient is by far the strongest partisan
signal. A 10-pp swing in Dem constituency → ~1.3× hazard of GBI entry,
~1.5× hazard of self-green entry. This is the cleanest evidence that the
electoral constituency channel matters more than the mayoral identity
channel at the first-entry margin.

### 3. Widmann's infrastructure-accumulation mechanism does NOT appear
within-state at the city level
`state_self_green_cum_count_lag1` and `state_any_rep_self_green_lag1`
both yield HR ≈ 1.00 with p ≈ 1.00, meaning state-level cumulative
municipal green-bond activity does **not** accelerate subsequent city-
level entry **within state strata**. This is a substantively important
null: Widmann's country-level infrastructure spillover (HR ≈ 1.017–1.021
per additional corporate bond) does not translate downward to within-
state city-level spillover in this panel.

**Two possible readings:**
1. **State strata absorb the cross-state variation** that carries the
   infrastructure signal. Cities in high-market states enter faster, but
   when you condition on state, there's no residual within-state
   temporal effect.
2. **The mechanism genuinely operates at a higher level.** Bank-
   mediated accumulation is country-wide; the within-state spillover from
   one muni issuer to the next is weak because lead-manager banks
   already have the expertise they need by the time the state market
   has any activity.

Both readings are consistent with Widmann — his mechanism is banks
transferring expertise across **corporate-sovereign boundary**, not
within-state muni-to-muni diffusion.

**Robustness to try:** re-run Cox WITHOUT state strata (cross-state
identification) to see if `state_self_green_cum_count_lag1` is
significant there. That would pinpoint whether state strata are
absorbing it.

### 4. City size dominates
`log_population_city_lag2` HR ≈ 2.17 (p < 0.001) in both Cox models.
Each log-unit bigger city has ~2× hazard of first entry. Strongest and
most precisely estimated covariate. Mirrors the LPM result but quantifies
it in hazard-rate terms.

| Corporate Private Cumulative | **1.021\*\*** per +1 bond | `state_self_green_cum_count_lag1` | **1.00 (ns)** |
| Agency Cumulative | **0.89\*\*** per +1 bond (substitutes) | `has_substitute_issuer` | not in focus — need dedicated test |
| GDP per cap. | 2.44\* to 2.61\*\* | `log_percapita_income_city_lag2` | 2.60 (p = 0.12) |
| Population | 2.39\*\* | `log_population_city_lag2` | **2.17\*\*\*** |

The scale/demographic covariates align substantively. The accumulation
covariate does not — a finding worth explicit discussion in the paper.

## What this adds to the paper

The Cox analysis gives you **one new appendix table** with these
takeaways:
- Confirms Dem_Mayor null finding under a survival-model framework
- Quantifies the constituency (pres_dem_share) effect in hazard terms
- Provides a direct Widmann comparability row (our state_self_green_cum
  is NOT significant, suggesting the bank-mediated diffusion operates
  at country scale, not state-within-country)

The null on state_self_green_cum is **interesting in itself** —
interpretation: Widmann's mechanism is about global-bank-mediated
cross-country diffusion, not intra-state spillover.

## Caveats

1. **Within-state stratification may be too tight.** 49 strata, 72
   events total. Some strata have 0 events and effectively drop. An
   alternative strata choice (population quintile, region, or unstratified
   with covariates) would produce different HRs for state-level variables.
2. **Recurrent issuance is ignored.** Cox A treats only the FIRST green
   bond per city. Cities that issue multiple times (NYC, Chicago, etc.)
   contribute one event. Andersen-Gill recurrent-events Cox is the
   natural extension if you want to preserve repeat-issuance information.
3. **State-level infrastructure variable scale.** `state_self_green_cum_count_lag1`
   has mean ~5 and max 32. Try the asinh-transformed version as a
   robustness to see if the HR is more interpretable.
