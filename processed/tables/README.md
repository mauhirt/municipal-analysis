**Stacked monotonicity test** (Col 9): interacting `Rep_Mayor_lag1` with a
compulsion ordinal (water=4 … green buildings=0) over all eight categories:

- N = 47,224
- β(compulsion_ord) = **−0.0145\*\*** (se 0.0061)
- β(Rep_Mayor × compulsion_ord) = **−0.0009\*** (se 0.0005)

The interaction is small and marginally significant. **Note:** the sign is
opposite to H2a's naive prediction — in the full unconditional sample the
sparse non-water categories dominate and the ordinal-by-partisanship pattern
flattens. The memo's cleanest empirical anchor remains the water-only Fisher
exact (reported under Table 3 below).

Signals worth noting at the column level:
- **Renewables** (muni-electric subsample, N=854): `ep_muni_electric_rev_asinh_lag1`
  is **−0.03\*** — deeper muni-electric operations are, if anything, negatively
  associated with renewable-energy green bonds, which cuts against a simple
  enterprise-fund-depth story.
- **Energy efficiency**: `ep_state_aceee_code_rank_lag1` is **−0.00\*\*** — a
  *better* ACEEE rank (lower number) is associated with *higher* efficiency-bond
  probability, the expected direction.
- **Climate adaptation**: `nri_inland_flooding_eal_bv` is negative and
  `fema_resil_grant_amt_asinh_lag1` is negative — both marginal. Physical-risk
  exposure does not obviously predict issuance conditional on controls.

See `table2_compulsion_gradient.md` and `table2_compulsion_gradient.csv`.

**Key reading:** the `Rep_Mayor` credibility gap is **remarkably stable** at
−0.21 to −0.24 across every new-variable perturbation. None of the new F3
variables (carbon pricing, underwriter block, anti-ESG institutional positions,
or federal grants) meaningfully attenuates the partisan gap. This is the
cleanest evidence yet of a sticky residual partisan effect at Step 4 —
consistent with H3b (market/electoral/peer disciplining forces fail to close
the gap).
