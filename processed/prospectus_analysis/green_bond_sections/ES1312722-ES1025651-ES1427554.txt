The City has designated the Project as a “Green Project” in con nection with the Certificates due 
to the sustainable design features which the City anticipates w ill result in LEED Gold Certification at 
completion. The sustainable design features of the Project include the following: 
• graywater treatment system that will help conserve fresh water; 
• use of View® Dynamic Glass windows, a glass product that utilizes electrochromic glazing 
and modulates the amount of solar energy entering the building to maximize daylight while 
reducing glare and heat; 
• over 2,000 curtainwall segments designed to increase thermal efficiency, reducing energy 
demand and carbon emissions; 
• solar roof panels; and 
• on-site Electric vehicle (EV) parking spaces with charging and bicycle parking. 
 
The City has a long-standing co mmitment to sustainability. The City has established the 
Department of the Environment to provide solutions that advance  climate protection and promote policies 
requiring energy efficiency improvement in existing buildings and new construction. The City, which first 
established municipal green building requirements by ordinance in 1999, has been a leader in green 
policies for both public and private sector buildings. The San Francisco Green Building Code (enacted by 
Ordinance No. 259-13 passed by the Board of Supervisors on Nove mber 5, 2013 and approved by the 
Mayor on November 14, 2013) sets green building requirements th at apply to all new construction in the 
City. In addition to the requirements of the San Francisco Gree n Building Code, city-owned facilities and 
leaseholds must comply with the Municipal Green Building Code ( Environment Code Chapter 7 Section 
706) which sets requirements for municipal construction projects with measures related to: 
• Minimum LEED (Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design) Gold Certification 
• Stormwater Management 
• Indoor Water Use Reduction  
• Renewable Energy, Energy Efficiency, Better Roofs, and Energy Resilience 
• Construction Debris and Waste Management 
• Indoor Air Quality 
• Low Emitting Materials 
• Toxics Reduction and Pollution Prevention
