ABOUT THE ISSUER  
The City of Atlanta (the “City”), the capital of and most populous city in the State of Georgia, is at the center 
of the eighth largest metropolitan statistical area in the United States based on the estimated population 
for 2024. The Hartsfield -Jackson Atlanta International Airport (the “Airport” or “ATL”), owned by the City  
and operated by the City of Atlanta Department of Aviation (the “Department of Aviation” or “Department”),

is the busiest  passenger airport in the world, serving more than 100 million passengers annually. 1 The 
Airport generates significant economic activity in the region and directly supports more than 63,000 jobs.2 
The Airport terminal complex comprises seven concourses connected by the underground Plane Train. 
The Airport also provides an international shuttle connector, ground transportation, a rental car center, the 
ATL SkyTrain, and access to public transportation through the Metropolitan Atlanta Rapid Transit Authority 
(MARTA). 
The Department of Aviation has robust commitments to decarbonization and environmental sustainability. 
ATL joined the Airport Council International Airport Carbon Accreditation (“ACA”) Program to validate 
Scope 1 and Scope 2 emissions inventories and conduct s greenhouse gas inventories regularly.  The 
Department is committed to achieve 100% clean and/or renewable energy by 2035 and net zero emissions 
by 2050. These targets align with those of the City. Strategies to meet clean energy and emission reduction 
goals are described in multiple sustainability policies.3  
In partnership with Georgia Power, the Department of Aviation implements energy conservation measures 
and renewable energy projects, and continually evaluates energy baselines  of facilities  to identify 
opportunities to improve energy efficiency . Construction projects meet green building standards and 
sustainable infrastructure standards. ATL was the first airport to earn a LEED Platinum Communities rating 
in 2019. 
Additionally, the Department of Aviation: 
• Sets water conservation  and waste reduction goals. For project data collected s ince 2016, the 
Department’s major capital projects have averaged a 40% reduction in water use and 87% diversion 
of construction and demolition waste from landfills.4 
• Completed a solar feasibility study to identify 55 potential sites for solar arrays  across the airport 
campus. As of July 2025, three sites have newly operational solar installations, and 14 more are in 
planning stages.  
• Has built infrastructure to support electrification of ground service equipment across the terminal 
complex. 
• Performs whole-building life cycle assessments on major capital projects to identify opportunities 
to reduce embodied emissions and environmental impacts.

ALIGNMENT TO GREEN STANDARDS 8 
Use of Proceeds  
A portion of the proceeds of the Series 2025B-1 Bonds will be used to finance and 
refinance short term debt obligations  that financed expansion, construction and 
renovation projects at Hartsfield -Jackson Atlanta International Airport  
(collectively, the “ATL Green Bond Projects” or “Projects”).  The bond -financed 
activities meet robust green building standards, advance long-term sustainability 
goals, and are eligible projects as defined by the Green Bond Principles in the 
project categories of Green Buildings and Energy Efficiency. 
The ATL Green Bond Projects include expansion of Concourse D, modernization of gates in Concourses T 
and E, and upgrades to boilers in the central utility plant to improve energy efficiency. All Projects are 
designed to meet at least LEED Silver standards and/or significantly reduce energy use. Projects are 
outlined in Table 1 and described in more detail below.

Concourse D Widening and SkyClub 
The Series 2025B-1 Bonds partially finance expansion of Concourse D, including widening from 60 feet to 
approximately 100 feet and modifying gate layouts. With the expansion, Concourse D will have 
approximately 34 gates, a 25,000 -square-foot area for a Delta Sky Club, and a n additional 
100,000 square feet of  space at the  boarding level, concession areas, restrooms and other space for 
passengers. Widening of Concourse  D will improve airport circulation . The project incorporates multiple 
green building features as described below. 
Concourse D widening is a multiphase project. Phase I is complete and comprises major structural 
elements, roofing and exterior enclosure. Phase II is ongoing as of July 2025 and involves major utility 
work, restrooms and interior finishes. Six more construction phases are planned for completion by summer 
2029. 
The entire Concourse D Widening project is designed to achieve LEED Silver certification and incorporates 
the following sustainable design elements: 
• Fully electrified gates 
• Efficient heating and cooling systems  
• Efficient, insulated wall panels 
• Low emissivity
