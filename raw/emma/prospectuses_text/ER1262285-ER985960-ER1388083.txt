The Indianapolis Local Public Improvement Bond Bank Bonds, Series 2019F (Stormwater Project) (the “2019 Bonds”) will be dated 
the date of initial delivery, and will bear interest from that date to their respective maturities in the amounts and at the rates set forth on 
the inside cover hereof. The 2019 Bonds are issuable only as fully registered bonds and, when issued, will be registered in the name of Cede 
& Co., as nominee for The Depository Trust Company, New York, New York (“DTC”). Purchases of beneficial interests in the 2019 Bonds 
will be made in book-entry-only form, in the denomination of $5,000 or any integral multiple thereof. Purchasers of beneficial interests in 
the 2019 Bonds (the “Beneficial Owners”) will not receive physical delivery of certificates representing their interests in the 2019 Bonds. 
Interest on the 2019 Bonds is payable on January 1 and July 1 of each year, commencing July 1, 2020. Interest, together with the principal 
and redemption premium, if any, of the 2019 Bonds, will be paid directly to DTC by U.S. Bank National Association, Indianapolis, Indiana, 
as paying agent (the “Paying Agent”) under the Indenture, as defined and described herein, so long as DTC or its nominee is the registered 
owner of the 2019 Bonds. The final disbursement of such payments to the Beneficial Owners of the 2019 Bonds will be the responsibility of 
the DTC Participants and the Indirect Participants, all as defined and more fully described herein under the caption “THE 2019 BONDS — 
Book-Entry-Only System.”

Bond Bank (the “Bond Bank” or “Issuer”) on September 16, 2019, and are issued pursuant to the provisions of a Trust Indenture dated as 
of October 1, 2019 (the “Indenture”), between the Bond Bank and U.S. Bank National Association, as trustee (the “Trustee”), and the laws 
of the State of Indiana, including particularly Indiana Code 5-1.4 (the “Act”). The Trustee will serve as the Registrar and Paying Agent (the 
“Registrar” or “Paying Agent” and together, the “Registrar and Paying Agent”) for the 2019 Bonds under the Indenture. The 2019 Bonds are 
issued for the principal purpose of providing funds to: (i) effect a current refunding of The Indianapolis Local Public Improvement Bond 
Bank Notes, Series 2016 (Stormwater Project), dated July 8, 2016, as amended and restated by the Amended and Restated The Indianapolis 
Local Public Improvement Bond Bank Notes, Series 2016 (Stormwater Project) (as amended and restated, the “Prior Notes”), and currently 
outstanding in the aggregate principal amount of $50,000,000, plus any accrued interest; (ii) pay for all or a portion of the costs of certain 
additions and improvements to the Qualified Entity’s Stormwater System (as defined herein) (the “2019 Projects”); and (iii) pay all costs 
incidental to, or on account of the issuance of, the 2019 Bonds and the 2019 Qualified Obligations (as defined herein).  The Prior Notes 
were issued to provide funds to (i) purchase the City of Indianapolis, Indiana, Stormwater District Notes of 2016, as amended and restated 
(as amended and restated, the “Original Qualified Obligations”), issued by the City of Indianapolis, Indiana (the “City”), for and on behalf 
of the Marion County Stormwater Management District (the “Qualified Entity” or “Stormwater District”), in order to pay for the costs of 
certain additions and improvements to the Qualified Entity’s Stormwater System, and (ii) pay the costs of issuance of the Prior Notes and 
the Original Qualified Obligations. See “FINANCING PLAN.”  The Bond Bank is a body corporate and politic, separate from the City, and 
established for the public purposes set forth in the Act. Pursuant to the Act, the purpose of the Bond Bank is to purchase and sell securities

THE STORMWATER SYSTEM 
Territory 
 
 Pursuant to the Stormwater Act and the Council Ordinance, the geographic boundaries of the Qualified 
Entity includes all of the County, except for the territory wit hin the City of Beech Grove, the Town of Speedway, 
the City of Southport (as of 2015), and the Town of Cumberland (each of which are “excluded cities and towns” 
under the Stormwater Act).  The territory within the corporate boundaries of the City of Beech Grove, the City of 
Southport, the Town of Speedway and the Town of Cumberland (com bined population of 34,592) are not included 
within the territory of the Qualified Entity, because each of such entities has either established their own stormwater 
district and/or statutorily withdrawn from the territory of the  Qualified Entity pursuant to the procedures set forth in 
the Stormwater Act. See “RISK FACTORS.” 
 
 The Qualified Entity encompasses approximately Three Hundred N inety-Eight (398) square miles and 
includes approximately Two Hundred Seventy Three Thousand Nine Hundred Eight (273,908) residential parcels 
and approximately Twenty-Five Thousand Two Hundred Sixty Eight (25,268) non-residential parcels (these include 
agricultural property, apartment complexes, commercial and indu strial properties, schools, churches and municipal 
and federal government facilities). The system infrastructure c onsists of drainage outfalls, flood management 
structures (levee and dams), pumping facilities, piped and open  channel conveyance systems and water quality 
treatment basins and other facilities. 
 
The Qualified Entity and its pre decessors have been maintaining  structures and assets since the 1950s.  In 
2012, the Qualified Entity completed a separate inventory and v aluation of assets using current replacement cost

Management and Operations 
 
 The Council Ordinance provides that the Qualified Entity’s pur pose is to provide for the safe and efficient 
capture and conveyance of stormwater runoff, mitigate the damag ing effects of stormwater runoff, correct 
stormwater collection and conveyance problems and fund the acti vities of stormwater management including, but 
not limited to, design, planning, regulatory compliance, educat ion, administration, construction, operation, 
maintenance, inspection and enforcement activities. 
 
 The Qualified Entity engages an outside consultant to assist w ith permit compliance monitoring and certain 
administrative functions, including billing and collection. Per mit compliance responsibilities include managing the 
City of Indianapolis’ National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System (“NPDES”) stormwater permit issued by the 
Indiana Department of Environmental Management (“IDEM”). This N PDES permit requires that the City reduce 
pollutants from the Stormwater System by implementing and complying with a variety of conditions. This is 
accomplished through public education and outreach, standards a nd specification development and implementation, 
construction permitting, post construction water quality contro ls, reduction of illicit discharges, proper management 
of City owned facilities and update and implementation of City policies and procedures. 
 
The Qualified Entity also engages an outside consultant to assi st in the planning, design and 
implementation of the Stormwater Master Plan.  This assistance includes identification of problem drainage areas, 
project development, planning, design and construction assistance. 
 
 Since its inception, the Qualified Entity has retained a third -party contractor to manage the billing and 
collection functions for non-taxable entities.  The Treasurer o f Marion County bills all taxable properties for the 
Stormwater User Fee once a year with payments being collected i n May and November through the property tax 
billing and collection process based on billing data provided by the Qualified Entity. 
 
Revenues and Fee Structure 
 
Revenues of the Qualified Entity are generated through the assessment and collection of user fees as 
provided for in the Stormwater Act and the Council Ordinance (“ Stormwater User Fee”). The Qualified Entity’s 
current fees are assessed on a per parcel basis. The fee for a particular parcel is dependent upon the amount of 
impervious surface area (“ISA”). T he ISA is measured by the Qua lified Entity utilizing aerial photography. This 
new billing system took effect on July 1, 2015, and provides fo r annual $0.05 increases each subsequent January. 
Beginning July 1, 2015, all parcels were subject to a Stormwate r User Fee which is proportional to the quantity of 
ISA located on the parcel.  Each parcel having any ISAs was assessed a fee of One Dollar and Ten Cents ($1.10) per 
month for each 1,000 square feet of ISA on the property.  Each 1,000 square feet of ISA on the property is a Base 
Billing Unit (“BBU”) and BBUs are rounded to the next highest w hole number for Stormwater User Fee billing 
purposes. The monthly Stormwater User Fee is the product of the  rate ($1.30 as of January 1, 2019) and the number 
of Base Billing Units (BBUs).  ISA is defined as an area that i s either paved or covered with buildings or materials, 
such that the infiltration of surface water into the soil is im peded. As an example, a parcel having 2,700 square feet 
of ISA would pay a Stormwater User Fee of $3.90 per month.  Onl y three and a half years (July 2015 to December 
2018) of information is provided below because prior to July 1,  2015, a different rate structure was used and the 
numbers are not comparable.  Information for 2014 and prior years is available through EMMA.

Capital Improvement Plan  
 
 In February 1998, IDEM issued an NPDES Stormwater Discharge Pe rmit to the City of Indianapolis. 
Under the terms of the permit, the City was to develop a stormw ater management program designed to improve the 
overall water quality of stormwater runoff. As a result, the De partment of Public Works commissioned the 
development of a County-wide stormwater master plan (the “Plan”). 
 
 In 2001, the initial Stormwater Master Plan was issued to address problems and issues related to poor 
drainage, flooding, water quality, stream protection and other environmental impacts. The Plan inventoried and 
prioritized the roughly twelve thousand five hundred (12,500) outstanding stormwater related complaints; ranked the 
severity of problems; developed solution concepts and prelimina ry cost estimates; and reviewed financing options. 
The report identified approximately Three Hundred Million Dolla rs ($300,000,000) of pro jects associated with 
about three hundred fifty (350) p rojects, ranging from simpler maintenance activities to long-term capital 
improvement projects. The Plan was adopted by the Board of Publ ic Works on July 25, 2001.  Subsequent updates 
to the Stormwater Master Plan have continued to identify needs,  which have considerably outpaced available funds 
necessary to address such needs.  In 2019 there were over 1,300  problem areas identified throughout Marion 
County.  These areas are being i nvestigated to identify whether they are issues for operations to resolve or if the 
problem requires a future capital investment. 
 
 The Qualified Entity’s 2020-2023 CIP estimates capital improve ments of over $134,000,000 will need to 
be made over the next four (4) years to comply with current regulatory requirements and to implement the Qualified 
Entity’s improvements to mitigate stormwater runoff management and water quality needs. The Qualified Entity 
currently expects to finance planned capital needs over the nex t four years from revenu es generated from the 
Stormwater System, short term notes and long-term bond proceeds and funds on hand.  The CIP references eight (8) 
main categories of expenditures and includes ‘Over-programming’ in the event projects need to be substituted and/or 
revenues exceed projections.

RISK FACTORS 
Bonds are Limited Obligations 
The 2019 Bonds are limited obligations of the Bond Bank payable  solely from the revenues and funds of 
the Bond Bank pledged therefor under the Indenture.  The 2019 B onds do not constitute a debt, liability or loan of 
the credit of the State or any political subdivision thereof, including the City, the Bond Bank, the Qualified Entity or  
 2 8 
any other qualified entity, under the constitution and the laws  of the State or a pledge of the faith, credit and taxing 
power of the State or any politi cal subdivision thereof, includ ing the City, the Bond Bank, the Qualified Entity or 
any other qualified entity. The Bond Bank has no taxing power.  Furthermore, the 2019 Bonds do not constitute a 
general or moral obligation of the Bond Bank or the City.  The Bond Bank will not maintain a debt service 
reserve fund for the 2019 Bonds and the provisions of Indiana C ode 5-1.4-5, pertaining to a moral obligation 
of the City-County Council to replenish a debt service reserve fund, do not apply to the 2019 Bonds.  See 
“SECURITY AND SOURCES OF PAYMENT FOR THE 2019 BONDS.” 
In addition to the authority to issue revenue bonds and set rat es and charges for the use of the Stormwater 
System, the Stormwater Act gives the Qualified Entity the legal  authority to issue General Obligation Bonds. 
However, both the City-County Council and the Board of Public Works, have made policy decisions not to levy any 
property taxes within the corporate boundaries of the Qualified  Entity, nor to operate the territory of the Qualified 
Entity as a special taxing district by issuing General Obligation Bonds for the Qualified Entity.  Therefore, no 
property taxes have been, or are expected to be, levied on prop erty within the territory of the Qualified Entity or are

A significant number of capital improvements undertaken by the Qualified Entity could be required in 
order for the Board of Public Works to continue to comply with environmental and other regulatory requirements.

to be made over the next three (3) years (2019, 2020, and 2021)  to comply with current regulatory requirements and 
to implement the Qualified Entity’s planned improvements to mit igate stormwater runoff management and water 
quality needs. The Qualified Entity  currently expects to financ e planned capital needs over the next three (3) years 
from bonds, revenues generated from the Stormwater System and f unds on hand. In the event the Qualified Entity 
determines to finance capital improvements through the issuance  of additional debt, such debt must be approved by 
the Board of Public Works and the City-County Council and compl y with the additional bonds test. Interest on such 
debt would increase debt service costs to the Qualified Entity and could result in lower debt service coverage or 
insufficient Net Revenues to pay such increased debt service co sts. See “APPENDIX C — Advisor’s Special 
Purpose Report” herein. The Qualified Entity must hold a public hearing before undertaking a new capital project, 
including the projects in the CIP. A successful legal objection to a new capital project could prevent its completion. 
Withdrawal from Territory of Qualified Entity 
 The territory of the Qualified Entity currently includes all o f the County, except for the territory within the 
City of Beech Grove, the Town of Speedway, the City of Southpor t, and the Town of Cumberland (each of which 
are “excluded cities and towns” under the Stormwater Act).  The  territory within the corporate boundaries of the 
City of Beech Grove, the Town of Speedway, the City of Southpor t, and the Town of Cumberland are not included 
within the territory of the Qualified Entity, because each of such entities had either established their own stormwater 
district at the time the Council O rdinance was adopted or have otherwise statutorily withdrawn from the territory of 
the Qualified Entity pursuant to the procedures set forth in the Stormwater Act.  Pursuant to the Stormwater Act, any 
excluded city or town in the County may, at any point in the fu ture, withdraw from the territory of the Qualified 
Entity pursuant to certain procedures set forth in the Stormwat er Act.  Currently, the City of Lawrence is the only 
other excluded city or town in the County which is included wit hin the territory of the Qualified Entity.  The City of 
Lawrence could elect to withdraw from the jurisdiction of the Q ualified Entity, pursuant to the provisions of the 
Stormwater Act, in which event the City of Lawrence would be li able for, and be required to pay, an allocable 
portion of the indebtedness of the Qualified Entity that is out standing at such time. There can be no assurances that 
the City of Lawrence will not el ect to withdraw from the territ ory of the Qualified Entity in the future while any 
portion of the 2019 Bonds remain outstanding.

In the opinion of Bingham Greenebaum Doll LLP, Indianapolis, Indiana (“Bond Counsel”), under existing 
laws, regulations, judicial decisions and rulings, interest on the 2019 Bonds is excludable from gross income for 
federal income tax purposes pursuant to Section 103 of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended and in effect 
on the date of issuance of the 2019 Bonds (the “Code”). Interes t on the 2019 Bonds is not a specific preference item 
for purposes of the federal alternative minimum tax. This opini on of Bond Counsel relates only to the exclusion 
from gross income of interest on the 2019 Bonds for federal income tax purposes under Section 103 of the Code and 
is conditioned on continuing compliance by the Bond Bank and Qu alified Entity with the Tax Covenants 
(hereinafter defined). Failure to comply with the Tax Covenants  could cause interest on the 2019 Bonds to lose the 
exclusion from gross income for federal income tax purposes retroactive to the date of issue. In the opinion of Bond 
Counsel, under existing laws, regulations, judicial decisions a nd rulings, interest on the 2019 Bonds is exempt from 
income taxation in the State of Indiana. See APPENDIX A hereto for the form of Bond Counsel opinion. 
The Code imposes certain requirements which must be met subsequ ent to the issuance of the 2019 Bonds 
as a condition to the exclusion from gross income of interest o n the 2019 Bonds for federal income tax purposes.

that would result in the loss of the exclusion from gross incom e for federal income tax purposes of interest on the 
2019 Bonds pursuant to Section 103 of the Code (collectively, t he “Tax Covenants”). The Indenture, the Bond 
Resolution and certain certificates and agreements to be delive red on the date of delivery of the 2019 Bonds

Indenture if interest on the 2019 Bonds is not excludable from gross income for federal tax purposes or otherwise 
pursuant to any provision of the Code which is not in effect on the date of issuance of the 2019 Bonds. 
Indiana Code 6-5.5 imposes a franchise tax on certain taxpayers  (as defined in Indiana Code 6-5.5) which, 
in general, includes all corporations which are transacting the  business of a financial institution in Indiana. The 
franchise tax is measured in part by interest excluded from gro ss income under Section 103 of the Code minus 
associated expenses disallowed under Section 265 of the Code.

Obligations.
