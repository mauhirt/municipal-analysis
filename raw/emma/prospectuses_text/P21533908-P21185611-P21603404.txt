Issuer of Bonds.  The Series 2022-A Bonds and Series 2022-B Bonds referred to above and collectively referred to hereinafter as the 
“Series 2022 Bonds” are being issued by South Dallas Water Authority, Inc., a public corporation under the laws of the State of Alabama (the 
“Authority”). 
Authorizing Documents.  The Series 2022 Bonds are being issued pursuant to a Trust Indenture dated as of January 25, 2022 (the 
“Indenture”), between the Authority and The Bank of New York Mellon Trust Company, N.A., as trustee (the “Trustee”), and an authorizing 
resolution (the “Authorizing Resolution”) adopted by the governing body of the Authority authorizing the issuance of the Series 2022 Bonds. 
Use of Proceeds .  Proceeds of the Series 2022-A Bonds will be used by the Authority to advance refund on a taxable basis the 
Authority’s Subordinated Water Revenue Bond, Series 2014-DWSRF-DL (the “SRF Bond”) and to finance the costs of issuance for the Series 
2022-A Bonds.  Proceeds of the Series 2022-B Bonds will  be used to currently refund on a tax-exempt basis the Authority’s Water Revenue 
Bond, Series 1989 (the “Series 1989 Bonds”), Water Revenue Bond, Series 2009-A (the “Series 2009-A Bond”), Water Revenue Bond, Series 
2009-B (the “Series 2009-B Bond”), and to finance the costs of issuance for the Series 2022-B Bonds. 
Source of Payment.  The Series 2022 Bonds are limited obligations of the Authority payable, on a parity of lien with any Additional 
Bonds (as defined in the Indenture) hereafter issued constituting future parity obligations, from the net revenues from the Authority’s waterworks 
distribution system and its sanitary sewer system (collectively, the “System”) remaining after payment of the reasonable and necessary costs of 
maintaining and operating the System.

Insurance.  The scheduled payment of principal of and interest on the Series 2022 Bonds when due will be guaranteed under a municipal bond 
insurance policy to be issued concurrently with the delivery of the Series 2022 Bonds by BUILD AMERICA MUTUAL ASSURANCE COMPANY. 
 
Form and Date of Delivery.  The Series 2022 Bonds are being initially issued in book entry form under the rules and regulations of 
The Depository Trust Company (“DTC”).  The Series 2022 Bonds are expected to be delivered on or about January 25, 2022. 
Legal Counsel.  Maynard Cooper & Gale, P.C. has served as bond counsel and disclosure counsel to the Authority and will deliver its 
opinion with respect to the Series 2022 Bonds in substantially the form attached as APPENDIX B.

INTRODUCTION 
 This Official Statement of the South Dallas Water Authority, Inc. (the “Authority”), and the appendices 
attached hereto are being furnished to provide certain information concerning the $385,000 aggregate principal 
amount Water and Sewer Revenue Bonds (Federally Taxable), Series 2022-A (the “Series 2022-A Bonds”) and 
$3,570,000 Water and Sewer Revenue Bonds, Series 2022-B (the “Series 2022-B Bonds”) (the Series 2022-A Bonds 
and Series 2022-B Bonds are collectively referred to hereinafter as the “Series 2022 Bonds”) issued by the Authority. 
 
 The Authority is a public corporation organized under the laws of the State of Alabama (the “State”) which 
owns and operates a water distribution system and a sanitary sewer system (as at any time constituted, collectively 
referred to as the “System”) located primarily in the southern portion of Dallas County, Alabama, and certain area in 
unincorporated Lowndes County, Alabama.  The Authority was originally incorporated in 1979 pursuant to the 
provisions of Chapter 88 of Title 11 of the CODE OF ALABAMA 1975, as amended. 
 
 The Series 2022 Bonds are being issued pursuant to a Trust Indenture dated as of January 25, 2022 (the 
“Indenture”), between the Authority and The Bank of New York Mellon Trust Company, N.A., as trustee (the 
“Trustee”). 
  
 The Series 2022-A Bonds are being issued for the purposes of: (i) advance refunding on a federally taxable 
basis the Authority’s SRF Bond, issued in the original aggregate principal amount of $490,000, and currently 
outstanding in the aggregate principal amount of $350,000; and (ii) paying the issuance costs for the Series 2022-A 
Bonds. 
 
 The Series 2022-B Bonds are being issued for the purposes of: (i) currently refunding on a tax-exempt basis 
the Authority’s Series 1989 Bond, issued in the original aggregate principal amount of $3,529,000, and currently 
outstanding in the aggregate principal amount of $441,000; (ii) currently refunding on a tax-exempt basis the 
Authority’s Series 2009-A Bond dated October 20, 2009, issued in the original aggregate principal amount of 
$2,610,000, and currently outstanding in the aggregate principal amount of $2,261,000; (iii) currently refunding on a 
tax-exempt basis the Authority’s Series 2009-B Bond dated October 20, 2009, issued in the original aggregate 
principal amount of $919,000, and currently outstanding in the aggregate principal amount of $779,000; and 
(iv) paying the issuance costs for the Series 2022-B Bonds. 
 
 The Series 2022 Bonds will not constitute general obligations of or a charge against the general credit or 
taxing power of the State of Alabama, or any county or any municipality.  The Authority has no taxing power.  The 
Series 2022 Bonds will be limited obligations of the Authority payable, on a parity of lien with any “Additional Bonds” 
(as defined in the Indenture) hereafter issued as parity obligations, solely out of and secured by a pledge of the revenues 
from the System remaining after payment of the reasonable and necessary expenses of maintaining and operating the 
 
 9 
System (the “Net System Revenues”) and a nonforeclosable statutory mortgage lien on the System.  See “S ECURITY 
FOR SERIES 2022 BONDS; SOURCE OF PAYMENT”.    
 
 The Authority also has outstanding a Water Revenue Bond, Series 2002-DWSRF-DL, outstanding in the 
approximate principal amount of $60,000 with a schedule maturity of February 1, 2023 (the “Series 2002 SRF Bond”) 
which the Authority plans to retire and defease from its own cash simultaneously with the issuance of the Series 2022 
Bonds.   
 
In the Indenture the Authority has reserved the right to issue additional bonds payable from and secured by

Book-Entry Only System; Registration, Payment and Transfer 
 The Depository Trust Company, New York, New York (“DTC”) will serve as initial Securities Depository 
under a Book-Entry System for the Series 2022 Bonds with no physical distribution of Series 2022 Bonds made to 
any owner of any Series 2022 Bond or Bonds.  The ownership of one fully registered Series 2022 Bond will be 
registered in the name of Cede & Co., as initial Securities Depository Nominee.  Except as provided below, so long 
as the Securities Depository or the Securities Depository Nominee is the registered owner of the Series 2022 Bonds, 
references herein to the holders, owners or registered owners of the Series 2022 Bonds shall mean the Securities 
Depository or the Securities Depository Nominee and shall not mean the Beneficial Owners (as defined below) of the 
Series 2022 Bonds. 
 
 DTC is a limited-purpose trust company organized under the New York Banking Law, a “banking 
corporation” under the New York Banking Law, a member of the Federal Reserve System, a “clearing corporation” 
within the meaning of the New York Uniform Commercial Code, and a “clearing agency” registered pursuant to the 
provisions of Section 17A of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934, as amended.  DTC was created to hold securities 
of its participants (the “Direct Participants”) and to facilitate the clearance and settlement of securities transactions, 
such as transfers and pledges, among Direct Participants in such securities through electronic computerized book-
entry changes in accounts of the Direct Participants, thereby eliminating the need for physical movement of securities 
certificates.  Direct Participants include securities brokers and dealers, banks, trust companies, clearing corporations, 
and certain other organizations.  DTC is owned by a number of Direct Participants and by the New York Stock 
Exchange, Inc., the American Stock Exchange, Inc., and the National Association of Securities Dealers, Inc.  Access 
to the Book-Entry System of DTC is also available to others such as banks, brokers, dealers and trust companies that 
clear through or maintain a custodial relationship with a Direct Participant, either directly or indirectly (the “Indirect 
Participants”).  The rules applicable to DTC and the Direct Participants are on file with the Securities and Exchange 
Commission.

THE AUTHORITY 
General 
 On May 14, 1979, the Authority was incorporated pursuant to enabling law now codified in Chapter 88 of 
Title 11 of the Code of Alabama, 1975, as amended.  The Authority currently provides water service to certain portions 
of the southern area of Dallas County and a small area in adjoining Lowndes County (the Authority has only 
approximately twenty customers within Lowndes County but the primary purpose of the Authority’s system in 
Lowndes County is to have an interconnector with Lowndes County Water Authority as an emergency supplier).  
 
The Authority’s water system (the “Water System”) was originally constructed using proceeds of loans made 
by the Farmers Home Administration of the United States of America dating back to 1979.   On November 15, 1989, 
the Authority amended its articles of incorporation to change its name from South Dallas Water and Fire Protection 
Authority to its current name of South Dallas Water Authority, Inc.   The Authority also serves an existing industrial 
park with approximately eight users with limited sewer service (the “Sewer System”). 
 
The Authority’s incorporation was consented to by the governing body of Dallas County, Alabama (the 
“County”) and the County is who appoints the directors to the Authority’s governing body.  The following individuals 
currently serve as the Board of Directors and/or officers of the Authority through the dates shown for their terms:

a certified Grade IV operator as a water specialist, who has served in that role to the Authority since 2007.

 The Authority currently serves approximately 1,180 customers with water service and approximately 8 sewer 
customers at an industrial park area.  Assuming a normal household contains 3 individuals on average, the approximate 
service area population of the Authority’s water system is 3,540. Not including service lines, the Authority has 
approximately 221 miles of pipe within its system with approximately 220 of said pipe being PVC pipe and the 
remaining pipe being cast iron or other material.  The distribution lines of the Authority’s water system range from 3” 
to 12” in diameter.    
 
Under its Enabling Law, the Authority may obtain the authority to provide water service, sewer service, and 
fire protection service; however, the Authority currently provides only water service except for certain limited sewer 
service as described below.    
 
Since 2003, the Authority has provided sanitary sewer service to a small number of users (less than ten users) 
located at a specific industrial park area.   The Authority has a servicing agreement (the “Sewer Agreement”) with a 
neighboring sanitary sewer system owned and operated by the Dallas County Water, Sewer and Fire Protection 
Authority.  Under the terms of the Sewer Agreement, the Authority owns a number of sewerage collection lines which 
tie with and connect with sewer lines of Dallas County Water, Sewer and Fire Protection Authority which allows for 
said sewerage to be properly treated by Dallas County Water, Sewer and Fire Protection Authority.   The Authority 
currently pays the Dallas County Water, Sewer and Fire Protection Authority a fixed fee of $1.00 of water sold (per 
1,000 gallons) to a user (by the Authority) as the fee for allowing the Authority to pass on the sewerage to the Dallas 
County Water, Sewer and Fire Protection Authority which accepts and treats said sewerage discharge from the 
industrial customers.  For fiscal year ending December 31, 2020, the Authority generated $36,476 in revenue from 
providing said sewer service and paid $30,029 in charges to the Dallas County Water, Sewer and Fire Protection 
Authority.  All of the Authority’s revenues are primarily derived from the sale of potable water to customers except 
for the limited sewer service revenue discussed above.   
 
The Authority’s water system is comprised mainly of two primary wells (referred to as the Justice Well with 
a 340 gallons per minute capacity and the Smith Wells with a 650 gallons per minute capacity), a 1,500 gallon per 
minute treatment plant (with treatment consisting of aeration with the oxidation of the iron and manganese and the 
addition of chlorine and disinfection and pressure sand filtration), four storage tanks with a combined capacity of 
1,700,000 gallons.  The Authority also has one hydro-pneumatic distribution pump station located in the Pleasant Hill 
community.  The Authority’s Water System has maximum gallon capacity of 1,425,600 gallons per day and, 
historically, the maximum number of gallons pumped in one day has been 577,000 gallons.  The Authority’s system 
is located in unincorporated Dallas County and a very small portion (principally dealing with having an 
interconnection point) in unincorporated Lowndes County—no part of the Authority’s system is inside any municipal 
limits, only unincorporated areas of Dallas County and Lowndes County, Alabama.   
 
The Authority largely produces its own water for its customers through its various wells and transports 
potable water through a network of distribution lines and storage tanks located near each of its wells.   The Authority 
has two primary sources of emergency back-up with Dallas County Water, Sewer and Fire Protection Authority and 
with Lowndes County Water Authority.  The Authority’s emergency back-up water supply agreement with Dallas 
County Water, Sewer and Fire Protection Authority is reciprocal in that either party can purchase water from the other 
party as needs dictate pursuant to a written agreement which extends through year 2045.

Billing Procedure 
 Meters are read between the 13 and 16 of the month every period and are generated once per month. 
 
Bills not paid by the due date have a 5% (or $4.00, whichever is greater) surcharge added as a late fee. Failure 
to pay a bill by the 2nd day of the following month after it is rendered results in a discontinuance of service. Procedures 
for discontinuance of water service are operable until all amounts owing and unpaid, including a reconnection charge 
of $60.00, have been paid in full or unless the customer has contacted the Authority to make special arrangements for 
payment.  Each customer pays a minimum of $50.00 deposit fee, unless a customer has been cut-off for nonpayment, 
in which case the security deposit is $100.00.  New connections to the System carry a $750.00 or greater tap fee.

 The Board of Directors of the Authority has sole jurisdiction to set the rates for water service to the System’s

Management and Personnel 
 
 Since around 2006, the Authority has employed ClearWater Solutions, LLC, an Alabama limited liability 
company (formerly known as Artesian Utility Systems Management, Inc.) (referred to herein as the “Third-Party 
Manager”) to manage and operate its System in its entirety.  The Authority has an existing management agreement 
with Clearwater Solutions, LLC, which extends through January 31, 2027, whereby the Authority has contractually 
agreed to pay the Third-Party Manager a fixed monthly fee (ranging from $29,819.06 per month to $32,584.09 per 
month) to operate, manage, and administer the System.   The Authority itself has no employees.  The Third-Party 
Manager provides its operational and administrative management services on an exclusive basis to the Authority.   The 
Authority’s compensation to its management system is a fixed fee and for fiscal years ending December 31, 2019 and 
2020, the management fees paid to the Third-Party Manager were $350,772 and $367,630, respectively.   
 
 The Authority does not have any employees of its own because it relies upon the Third Party Manager to 
provide all need labor and administrative services.  If the Authority were to ever terminate its management agreement 
with the Third Party Manager, it would presumably have to hire its own employees and administrators to operate and 
manage the Authority’s System.  
 
 The Authority management system, Clearwater Solutions, currently utilizes approximately six (6) full-time 
persons and three (3) part-term persons to operate the System, however said employees (of the Third-Party Manager) 
have over-lapping shared duties with West Dallas Water Authority, a neighboring water system also managed by the 
Third-Party Manager. The benefits and compensation for all employees of the Third-Party Manager are established 
by the governing body of the Third-Party Manager (not the Authority).  
 
 
Water Use Regulation 
 The Alabama Water Resources Commission has the power, after studies and findings respecting prospective 
shortages of water in any area of the State of Alabama, to adopt rules and regulations to impose restraints on 
withdrawals of water or sales of water in those areas. Its decision of necessity for such action must be reviewed each 
twelve months. The exact scope of operations and utilization of the powers granted to the Commission by the said 
legislation have not yet been determined. It is clear that the Authority will be entitled to participate in a public hearing 
before any such determination for the need of restriction of use became final. The Authority will, in a timely manner, 
file all required declarations of beneficial use and all amendments thereto and all annual reports in connection 
therewith which may be required by any statute, rule or regulation with respect to the Alabama Water Resources 
Commission or the Office of Water Resources of the Alabama Department of Economic and Community Affairs or 
any other body regulating the withdrawal and sale of water in the State of Alabama and having jurisdiction over the 
Authority. The Authority has the opportunity to participate in all public hearings which may be held which might have 
the result of restricting the withdrawal or sale of water in the Authority’s service area or to the Authority’s customers.

Transportation

Original Issue Discount.   Certain of the Series 2022-B Bonds are being sold with original issue discount. 
The original issue discount in the selling price of a bond, to the extent properly allocable to each owner of such bond, 
is excluded from gross income for federal income tax purposes with respect to such owner.  The original issue discount 
is the excess of the stated redemption price at maturity of such bond over the initial offering price to the public, 
excluding underwriters and other intermediaries, at which price a substantial amount of the bonds of such maturity 
were sold. 
 
Under Section 1288 of the Internal Revenue Code, original issue discount on tax-exempt bonds accrues on a 
compound basis.  The amount of original issue discount that accrues to an owner of a bond during any accrual period 
generally equals (i) the issue price of such bond plus the amount of original issue discount accrued in all prior accrual 
periods, multiplied by (ii) the yield to maturity of such bond (determined on the basis of compounding at the close of 
each accrual period and properly adjusted for the length of the accrual period), minus (iii) any interest payable on such 
bond during such accrual period.  The amount of original issue discount so accrued in a particular accrual period will 
be considered to be received ratably on each day of the accrual period, will be excluded from gross income for federal 
income tax purposes, and will increase the owner’s tax basis in such bond.  Purchasers of any bond at an original issue 
discount should consult their tax advisers regarding the determination and treatment of original issue discount for 
federal income tax purposes, and with respect to state and local tax consequences of owning such bonds. 
 
Original Issue Premium .  Certain of the Series 2022-B Bonds are being sold with original issue premium.  
An amount equal to the excess of the purchase price of a bond over its stated redemption price at maturity constitutes 
premium on such bond.  A purchaser of a bond must amortize any premium over such bond’s term using constant 
yield principles, based on the bond’s yield to maturity.  As premium is amortized, the purchaser’s basis in such bond 
and the amount of tax-exempt interest received will be reduced by the amount of amortizable premium properly 
allocable to such purchaser.  This will result in an increase in the gain (or decrease in the loss) to be recognized for 
federal income tax purposes on sale or disposition of such bond prior to its maturity.  Even though the purchaser’s 
basis is reduced, no federal income tax deduction is allowed.  Purchasers of any bonds at a premium, whether at the 
time of initial issuance or subsequent thereto, should consult with their own tax advisors with respect to the 
determination and treatment of premium for federal income tax purposes, and with respect to state and local tax 
consequences of owning such bonds.

FEDERAL BANKRUPTCY CODE 
 The remedies available to the Trustee or the registered owners of the Series 2022 Bonds upon an Event of 
Default under the Indenture are in many respects dependent upon judicial action which is often subject to discretion 
and delay.  Under existing constitutional and statutory law and judicial decisions, including specifically Title 11 of 
the United States Code (the federal bankruptcy code), the remedies provided in the Indenture may not be readily 
available or may be limited. 
 
 The rights and remedies of the registered owners of the Series 2022 Bonds are subject to the provisions of 
Chapter 9 of Title 11 of the United States Code (Bankruptcy).  Title 11 permits under certain specific circumstances 
(but only after authorization by the legislature or by a governmental officer or organization empowered by state law 
to give such authorization), a political subdivision of a state, such as the Authority, to file a petition for relief in the 
U.S. District Court (the “Court”) for the district in which the political subdivision is located if it is insolvent or unable 
to meet its debts as they mature and desires to effect a plan to adjust its debts.  Under the Bankruptcy Code, the filing 
of such a petition operates as an “automatic” stay of the commencement or the continuation of any judicial or other 
proceeding against the petitioner, its property or any officer or inhabitant of the petitioner.  Chapter 9 also permits a 
political subdivision that files such a petition to issue, with the approval of the Court, certificate of indebtedness having 
priority over pre-existing obligations. 
 
 Any political subdivision filing a petition for relief under Chapter 9 must in due course file a plan for the 
adjustment of its debts, and such plan may include provisions modifying or altering the rights of creditors generally, 
or any class of them, secured and unsecured.  Such plan, when confirmed by the Court, binds all creditors who had 
timely notice or actual knowledge of the petition or plan and discharges all claims against the petitioning political 
subdivision provided for in the plan.  No plan may, however, be confirmed by the Court unless certain conditions 
occur, which include either (1) that the plan has been accepted in writing by two-thirds (2/3) in the amount and more 
than fifty percent (50%) in number of the allowed claims of each class which is impaired by the plan, or (2) the Court 
finds that the plan does not discriminate unfairly, and is fair and equitable, with respect to each class of claims that is 
impaired under, and has not accepted, the plan. 
 
 Prospective purchasers of the Series 2022 Bonds should assume that existing Alabama statutes authorize the 
Authority to file a petition for relief under Chapter 9 of Title 11 of the United States Code.

RISK FACTORS 
 An investment in the Series 2022 Bonds involves certain risks which should be carefully considered by 
investors.  Prospective investors should carefully examine this Official Statement and their own financial condition in 
order to make a judgment as to their ability to bear the economic risk of such an investment and whether or not the 
Series 2022 Bonds are an appropriate investment for them.  Such risks include the following, among others: 
 
 1. The sufficiency of revenues to pay debt service on the Series 2022 Bonds may be affected by events 
and conditions relating generally to, among other things, population trends and economic developments in the service 
area of the System, litigation, the availability of water to the Authority, federal and state environmental regulations, 
the rate of return on investments in the Reserve Fund, the amount of moneys on deposit therein, and other 
circumstances, the exact nature and extent of which are not presently determinable. 
 
 2. The ability of the Authority to pay debt service on the Series 2022 Bonds and to comply with its 
rate covenant is based on certain projections and estimates of revenues to be derived from the System and estimates 
as to certain levels of operating expenses.  There is no assurance that such projections and estimates will be realized. 
 
 3. Recent studies conducted by various study teams, task forces and other state related agencies have 
indicated that the quantity of potable water in Alabama is less than has been available in earlier years and, in the future, 
restrictions on use of water, availability of water supplies and other such matters which could have an adverse impact 
on the revenues of the Authority and more restrictive rules as to usage may be imposed by state or federal regulatory 
agencies. 
 
 4. More stringent standards of water quality and testing to assure such standards are maintained than 
have heretofore been applicable may impose additional costs on the Authority in the treatment of water prior to its 
sale and increase the cost of water to the Authority.  This may reduce the amount of revenues of the Authority available 
for debt service to the extent it is not able to pass those increased costs on to its customers through increases in its 
rates. 
 
5.        The Authority faces risks of competition for customers from surrounding water systems. 
 
6. For more than a year, the world has recently experienced a wide-spread health pandemic or outbreak 
which could adversely impact the world economy and the economies of the United States, the State of Alabama, and 
the municipalities and service areas served by the Authority.  In December 2019, a novel strain of coronavirus 
(COVID-19) emerged in Wuhan, Hubei Province, China.  While initially the outbreak was largely concentrated in 
China and caused significant disruptions to its economy, it has since spread to many other countries and infections 
have been reported globally, including the United States and the State of Alabama.  The continued spread of the 
coronavirus in the United States and the State of Alabama could have a material adverse effect on the Authority’s 
operations and its financial condition, including a negative impact on the Authority’s ability to timely collect from 
water customers (on the basis that the Authority’s water customers may be temporarily unemployed or unable to 
generate revenue due to the State-imposed governmental shutdown).  The extent to which the coronavirus impacts the 
Authority’s operations and its financial condition will depend on future developments, which are highly uncertain and 
cannot be predicted with confidence, including the duration of the outbreak, new information which emerges 
concerning the severity of the disease and the actions to contain the coronavirus (including shutdown of business and 
social activities) or treat its impact, among others. 
 
7. Despite the implementation of network security measures by the Authority, its information 
technology systems may be vulnerable to breaches, hacker and ransomware attacks, computer viruses, physical or 
electronic break-ins and other similar events or issues.  State and local governments have recently been subject to such 
attacks.  The foregoing events or issues could lead to the disclosure of confidential information, ransomware attacks 
holding critical information and operations hostage or could have an adverse effect on the Authority’s ability to 
provide water service or to collect water revenues.  No assurances can be given that the Authority’s measures will 
prevent cybersecurity attacks, and no assurances can be given that any cybersecurity attacks, if successful, will not 
have a material adverse effect on the operations or financial condition of the Authority.   The Authority currently

Flow of Funds 
 
 The Indenture establishes the following funds for the collection and distribution of System revenues: 
 
 Revenue Fund. All System revenues will be deposited as received in the Revenue Fund.  The Authority will 
apply money on deposit in the Revenue Fund during each calendar month as follows: 
 
First Deposit in the Bond Fund the amounts required by the Indenture. 
 
Second Deposit in the Reserve Fund the amount required by the Indenture. 
 
 Bond Fund.  The Authority shall transfer from the Revenue Fund to the Bond Fund each month 1/6 of the 
interest coming due on the Bonds on the next ensuing interest payment date and 1/12 of the principal maturing or 
becoming subject to mandatory redemption on the Bonds on the next succeeding principal payment or mandatory 
redemption date. Money in the Bond Fund is to be applied solely for the payment of the principal of and interest on 
the Bonds as they respectively mature, become subject to mandatory redemption, and come due. 
  
 B-4 
 Reserve Fund.  On the date of delivery of the Series 2022 Bonds the Authority will deposit to the Reserve 
Fund in the form of (i) cash; (ii) a surety bond from Insurer; or (iii) any combination of the foregoing, so that the 
amount on deposit therein (or the amount covered by the surety bond) shall be equal to the Maximum Reserve 
Requirement. So long as and at any time the amount in the Reserve Fund is less than the Maximum Reserve 
Requirement (either in the form of cash or the face amount of reserve fund surety bond from Insurer), the Authority 
shall immediately transfer each month all moneys remaining in the Revenue Fund to the Reserve Fund (once the 
Authority’s obligations are satisfied with respect to the Operation Fund and Bond Fund) in not more than sixty monthly 
installments until the balance in the Reserve Fund (either in the form of cash or face amount of a reserve fund surety 
bond, or any combination of the foregoing) equals the Maximum Reserve Requirement. Money in the Reserve Fund 
is to be used to pay principal and interest on the Bonds, but only in the event that, at the time of any principal or 
interest payment date, money then held in the Bond Fund is insufficient for such purpose. 
 
 Surplus Revenues.  Any revenues remaining in the Revenue Fund on the last business day of each calendar 
month after making the aforesaid payments into the Bond Fund and the Reserve Fund shall be deemed “surplus 
revenues”. Such surplus revenues shall be released to the Authority and used for any lawful purpose. 
 
Depositories for Special Funds 
 
 The Trustee is designated in the Indenture as depository and custodian for the Bond Fund and the Reserve 
Fund. The depository and custodian for the Revenue Fund and the Operation Fund may be any banking institution 
designated by the Authority and approved by the Trustee that is qualified to do business in the State of Alabama and 
which is a member of the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation. 
 
Investment of Money in Special Funds 
 
 Money in the Revenue Fund, the Bond Fund and the Reserve Fund may be invested at the direction of the 
Authority in Federal Securities and/or Secured Deposits. All income derived from the investment of money on deposit 
in any such fund shall remain in the fund where earned; provided that so long as the balance in the Reserve Fund is 
equal to the Maximum Reserve Requirement, any income derived from the investment of the Reserve Fund shall be 
transferred to the Interest Account of the Bond Fund.  
 
Particular Covenants of the Authority 
 
 The Indenture will contain, among others, the following covenants of the Authority: 
 
 Maintenance of Books and Records; Annual Audits .  The Authority will maintain complete and accurate 
books and records respecting the operation of the System and will cause such books and records to be audited annually 
by an independent certified public accountant. 
 
 No Free Service.  The Authority will not furnish or permit to be furnished by or from the System any free 
service of any kind except public fire departments. All services furnished from the System will be charged for at the 
rates at the times established therefor. 
 
 Maintenance of Rates.  The Authority will make and maintain such rates and charges for the water services 
supplied by the System as shall be reasonably expected to produce revenues sufficient to pay all Operating Expenses 
when due and to produce Annual Net Income at least 1.10 times greater than the maximum Annual Net Debt Service 
Requirement. 
 
 Priority of Pledge .  The pledge of the revenues made by the Indenture shall be prior and superior to any

than with respect to any Additional Bonds hereafter issued which shall be on a parity with the Series 2022 Bonds with 
respect to the pledge by the Authority of the Net System Revenues. 
 
 Merger, Consolidation and Transfer of Assets.  The Authority shall not consolidate with or merge into any 
public corporation or transfer the System as an entirety to another public corporation.

 The Authority will take out and continuously maintain in effect insurance with respect to the System against

Default and Remedies 
 
 A default results under the Indenture from (a) the failure by the Authority to pay, when due, the principal of, 
premium (if any) and interest on any Bond; (b) the failure by the Authority to perform any of its other obligations 
under the Indenture if, after 30 days’ written notice to it of such failure made by the Trustee or by registered owners 
of more than 50% of bondholders, it has not remedied such default; or (c) the dissolution or liquidation of the Authority, 
the appointment of a receiver for the Authority, and other similar events specified in the Indenture. 
 
 The Indenture provides that the Trustee is empowered (and shall, if more than fifty percent of bondholders 
instruct (based on percentage ownership)), upon a default, to accelerate the maturity of all of the Bonds then 
outstanding and to institute legal and equitable proceedings to enforce and protect the rights of the bondholders.  
 
 Remedies are vested in the Trustee, not the bondholders, except under limited circumstances stated in the 
Indenture.
